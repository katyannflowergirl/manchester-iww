Why join the IWW?

The Industrial Workers of the World is a revolutionary global union, fighting for better conditions today and economic democracy tomorrow. We are a grass roots, democratic union, ran directly by the rank and file members, not union bureaucrats. We don't sell insurance or credit cards. But we're fighting and winning.

We teach all our members powerful organising tools, basic labour law, direct democracy and the creative direct action methods which give power back to the workers. If you're interested in attending a training session, get in touch!

We rely on each other, on the solidarity of our movement. Our members – from cleaners to teachers, shop and bar staff to call centre workers, factory workers to carers to more – are putting fear aside to take action other unions say is impossible.

But we want more. We want the economy run for the benefit of everybody, not a handful of executives on unimaginable pay. For this to happen, we need to be united into one movement, one big union, in every section of the economy. For this reason, we encourage existing union members, students and unemployed workers to also join the IWW, chipping in skills, time and experience to support other struggles and coming together across our industries regardless of other union divisions.

Join the “One Big Union” movement today.

We are:

  • Led by membership. We make all decisions and we all have the final say.
  • For uniting all workers across trades, industries and countries.
  • Able to offer practical support for members in their workplace.
  • Flexible, so you are still a member even when you change job or contract.

We are not:

  • Full of stifling bureaucracy or linked to any political party or group.
  • Led by fat cat salary earners who carry out deals with bosses behind your back.
  • Going to sell you services, life insurance or credit cards.

Who is the IWW for?

We are for ALL workers who do not have the power to hire or fire. This also includes workers who are retired, students, unemployed, part-time, temporary or those working at home. Workers who are members of other unions are also welcome.
